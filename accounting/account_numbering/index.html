    7-Digit Account Numbering Strategy¶

    Overview¶

    This document describes the 7-digit account numbering strategy used in our hierarchical Chart of Accounts. Each account number is exactly 7 digits long and is structured to represent up to 5 hierarchical levels. The 7-digit format allows us to easily categorize and identify accounts based on their level within the hierarchy.

    Structure of the 7-Digit Account Number¶

    Each account number is structured as follows:

    Digit Position Level Description
    1 Level 1 Represents the main category (1 digit)
    2-3 Level 2 Represents the sub-category (2 digits)
    4-5 Level 3 Represents a further sub-division (2 digits)
    6 Level 4 Represents a detailed level (1 digit)
    7 Level 5 Represents the most specific account (1 digit)

    Digit Breakdown¶

    1. Level 1 (1 digit):

    2. The first digit represents the highest level in the account hierarchy.

    3. It is a single digit number ranging from 1-9 and represents broad categories such as Assets, Liabilities, Revenue, Expenses, etc.

    4. Example: If the first digit is 1, it might represent Assets.

    5. Level 2 (2 digits):

    6. The second and third digits represent the sub-category within the main category.

    7. These digits provide further breakdown, typically representing specific sub-categories like Current Assets, Fixed Assets, or Accounts Receivable under Assets.
    8. This level uses two digits ranging from 00-99 to account for sub-categories.

    9. Example: If the digits are 01, it might represent Current Assets under Assets.

    10. Level 3 (2 digits):

    11. The fourth and fifth digits provide more detailed sub-categories within the Level 2 sub-category.

    12. These two digits further break down the account into specific groups, such as Cash, Bank Accounts, or Accounts Receivable.
    13. The digits range from 00-99.

    14. Example: If the digits are 01, it might represent Cash Accounts within Current Assets.

    15. Level 4 (1 digit):

    16. The sixth digit represents more granular details of the account.

    17. It is a single digit ranging from 0-9, and might be used to represent a specific bank account or a type of asset under a broader category.

    18. Example: If the digit is 1, it might represent a specific bank under Cash Accounts.

    19. Level 5 (1 digit):

    20. The seventh and final digit represents the most detailed level of the account, such as a specific account within a bank, or a further sub-division of assets.
    21. It is also a single digit ranging from 0-9.
    22. Example: If the digit is 1, it might represent a specific savings account at the bank under Bank Accounts.

    Example Account Number Breakdown¶

    Let’s look at an example to see how a 7-digit account number is structured:

    Account Number: 1010111

    Digit Position Digit Level Description
    1 1 Level 1 Represents Assets
    2-3 01 Level 2 Represents Current Assets
    4-5 01 Level 3 Represents Cash and Cash Equivalents
    6 1 Level 4 Represents a specific bank under Cash
    7 1 Level 5 Represents a specific bank account

    How Accounts Are Structured¶

    The 7-digit structure allows us to classify accounts in a clear, organized way:

    1. High-Level Categories (Level 1):
    2. Assets, Liabilities, Revenue, Expenses, Equity, etc.
    3. Sub-Categories (Level 2):
    4. Current Assets, Fixed Assets, Current Liabilities, Long-term Liabilities, Operating Revenues, Non-operating Revenues, etc.
    5. Sub-Divisions (Level 3):
    6. Cash, Accounts Receivable, Inventory, Trade Payables, Sales Revenue, etc.
    7. Detailed Levels (Level 4 & 5):
    8. Further breakdown for specific types of accounts, bank accounts, specific customers, or other specific financial details.

    Summary of the 7-Digit Account Numbering Strategy¶

    The 7-digit account number system is designed to create a hierarchical structure for classifying financial accounts. It ensures a consistent format while allowing sufficient granularity for detailed reporting. The structure is flexible enough to accommodate future expansion with new sub-categories or accounts, and can be easily integrated into financial systems.

    This structure helps to:

    • Maintain a clear and organized Chart of Accounts.
    • Allow hierarchical reporting and drill-down capabilities.
    • Easily identify and classify different levels of financial details.

    Explanation of Account Numbers¶

    • Assets (1xxxxxx):

    • Current Assets (101xxxx): Sub-category under assets.

    • Cash and Cash Equivalents (10101xx): Sub-division under current assets.

    • HDFC Bank Ltd (1010111): A specific account under cash and cash equivalents.

    • Liabilities (2xxxxxx):

    • Current Liabilities (201xxxx): Sub-category under liabilities.

    • Trade Payables (20101xx): Sub-division under current liabilities.

    • Other Payables (20102xx): Another sub-division under current liabilities.

    • Equity (3xxxxxx):

    • Share Capital (301xxxx): A major category under equity.

    • Retained Earnings (302xxxx): Another major category under equity.

    • Revenue (4xxxxxx):

    • Sales Revenue (401xxxx): Major source of revenue.

    • Service Revenue (402xxxx): Another source of revenue.

    • Expenses (5xxxxxx):

    • Cost of Goods Sold (501xxxx): Expense related to producing goods.
    • Salaries Expense (502xxxx): Salary expenses under the expenses category.
    • Rent Expense (503xxxx): Rent expenses.

    Summary¶

    This sample chart of accounts uses a 7-digit numbering system to represent different levels in the hierarchy, allowing each account to be easily categorized. Each level is reflected in the account number, starting from Level 1 (main category) and proceeding down to Level 5 (most specific accounts). The system ensures that the chart of accounts is clear, well-organized, and easy to navigate.
